# Public Onboarding Guide

This guide explains the customer onboarding flow at a high level.

## Step 1: Visit The Parent Website

The customer starts at:

- [www.vayunetrix.com](https://www.vayunetrix.com)

The parent website is the commercial and identity entry point for VayuNetrix products.

## Step 2: Register And Confirm Email

The customer creates a VayuNetrix account and confirms their email before product access is granted.

## Step 3: Choose AWS Platform Engineering

From the customer command center, the customer chooses AWS Platform Engineering.

## Step 4: Select Package And Capabilities

The customer selects the package and capability set they need. Examples include basic AWS foundation, governance/security, drift/remediation, and cost governance.

## Step 5: Review Quote, Order, And Subscription Status

The parent website owns the quote/order/subscription state. The AWS Platform portal receives only the entitlement summary it needs to show allowed capabilities.

## Step 6: Launch AWS Platform Portal

The parent website creates a signed product handoff and opens:

- [aws-platform.vayunetrix.com](https://aws-platform.vayunetrix.com)

The AWS Platform portal links the handoff to the correct workspace and opens Workspace Home.

## Step 7: Connect Workspace When Execution Is Needed

The customer prepares a local workspace and connector environment. Sensitive files remain local.

## Step 8: Provide Intake

The initial intake source may be an Excel workbook or guided portal form, depending on package and maturity.

## Step 9: Validate And Review

The platform validates intake, package entitlements, governance requirements, and planned changes.

## Step 10: Approve

The customer reviews plans, impact, cost, security, and blast-radius summaries before approving changes.

## Step 11: Execute Through Guarded Workflow

Approved workflows run through the connector and execution plane. The portal shows sanitized live status.

## Step 12: Review Evidence And Operate Continuously

The customer reviews evidence packs, governance status, security posture, drift reports, remediation outcomes, and cost summaries.
